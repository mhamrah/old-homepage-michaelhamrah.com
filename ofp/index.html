Optimum Fetal Position

Cliff Notes

What is it?

Mother's position and movement can influence baby in womb during final weeks.

You want baby in a good position to faciliate birth

Good: Occiput Anterior

Baby fits through pelvis as easily as possible

Head down, facing your back

Baby's back is on one side of tummy

Baby can easily flex head

Bad: Occiput Posterior

Head down, but facing tummy

Long, painful labor

Baby cannot fully flex head

Babies don't descend into pelvis before labor starts

Recent increase in # of cases

What to do?

Pay attention to posture when baby's head descends into pelvis.

Last six weeks of first pregnancy, 2-3 weeks second+.

Stay active and practice good posture.

Avoid comfortable sofas.

Try and sense baby's position

Good position is when the back feels hard and smooth

You feel kicks under ribs, your belly button pokes out, feels firm.

Flat, squishy stomach means bad position, kicks in stomach

Practice

Baby's back is heaviest part

Keep stomach lower then your back: lean forward

Avoid leaning back in armchairs, etc which put stomach high

Sleep on side, sit on wedge cushion in car, use yoga positions

If Already Posterior...

Sway hips while on hands and knees

Stick bottum up when on hands and knees

Try sleeping on tummy with lots of pillows

Rock side to side (if in labor)

Walk up stairs (if in labor)
